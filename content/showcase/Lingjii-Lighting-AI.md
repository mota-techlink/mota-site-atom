### Market Context: When Lighting Became an AI Product

The "big floor lamp" category recorded over **50% year-on-year growth for three consecutive years**—a trajectory that has reshaped how brands position smart lighting. This growth is not driven primarily by illumination quality but by the intelligence layer above the light. In consumer surveys, **68% of parents now rank "eye protection + AI" as the top purchase criterion**, ahead of price and design.

The data signals a structural shift: AI has moved from an optional differentiator to a required feature in mainstream products. The purchasing conversation is no longer "does it have voice control?" but "which AI is it running, and what can it actually do?" Brands that cannot answer this question clearly are already losing shelf space.

The industry's longstanding pain points make this shift urgent:

*   **Primitive control paradigms**: Traditional smart-lighting "intelligence" amounts to preset scenes and basic scheduling. Commands are static; they do not adapt to the user's activity, time of day, or environmental conditions.
*   **No educational or developmental layer**: Lamps sit in children's bedrooms and study rooms for thousands of hours per year, yet the intelligence they deliver stops at color temperature adjustment.
*   **No wellness dimension**: Light profoundly affects mood and cognitive state. None of the current hardware platforms have a systematic mechanism for translating that knowledge into real-time behavioral intervention.
*   **Fragmented ecosystems**: Parental management tools, educational content, and ambient control exist in separate apps with no shared context.

### Architecture: IOT + Multi-LLM + Vertical Models

Agility AI's architecture stacks three layers:

**Layer 1 — IOT Management**
All hardware interactions—switching, dimming, color temperature, mode selection, scheduling—are abstracted into a unified IoT management layer. This layer handles voice control, touchscreen input, and mobile app commands interchangeably, and exposes standardized APIs that the AI layer queries and instructs.

**Layer 2 — Multi-LLM Routing**
Rather than betting on a single model provider, the platform integrates multiple large model backends: **GPT-4, Gemini 1.5, and DeepSeek**, with routing logic that assigns requests to the most appropriate model based on task type, latency requirement, and cost. General knowledge queries, creative content, and complex reasoning tasks are distributed across models intelligently.

**Layer 3 — Vertical Models**
General-purpose LLMs cannot deliver the precision required for the platform's highest-value features. Two purpose-built vertical models are deployed alongside the general LLMs:
*   **Education & Curriculum Model**: Trained on pedagogical content for Chinese K-12, including grading logic, oral English evaluation rubrics, and age-appropriate learning material taxonomies.
*   **Psychology & Counseling Model**: Trained on behavioral and emotional intervention frameworks, enabling non-obvious detection of distress signals and generation of contextually appropriate therapeutic responses.

### Intelligence Features

#### Adaptive Environmental Control
The device continuously monitors two inputs: **human body presence detection** (via built-in sensor) and **ambient light levels** (via photodetector). The AI combines these signals with a third input—**real-time weather data via plugin**—to adjust lighting mode automatically. A cloudy afternoon during study time triggers a different color temperature profile than a clear evening during leisure.

Pomodoro timer integration goes further: structured work/rest cycles are supported directly in the device, with automatic lighting shifts at interval boundaries—bright cool-white for focus periods, warm amber for rest.

#### Intelligent Schedule Management
Weekly and monthly usage reports are generated and delivered via the companion app, giving parents visibility into how much time children spend under the lamp, what modes were active, and flagging irregular usage patterns. Schedule management overlays configurable "study hours" and "sleep preparation" windows that automatically enforce lighting boundaries.

### AI Education Suite

The education feature set targets the hours children spend at desks—arguably the highest-stakes lighting use case in any household. Three modules are deployed:

**Content Library**
A curated library of nursery rhymes, short stories, ancient Chinese poems, and oral English exercises, organized by age group and school curriculum stage. Content is delivered via TTS with the device's customizable voice, and can be navigated entirely by voice command.

**English Oral Evaluation**
Children practice spoken English; the ASR layer captures and transcribes the output; the Education Vertical Model scores pronunciation, fluency, and accuracy against grade-appropriate rubrics; a graded report is generated per session. This brings a feature previously available only through dedicated English learning apps or live tutoring into the ambient lamp interface.

**Study Supervision + Partnership**
*Supervision mode* activates the onboard camera to monitor the child's posture and attention state. The AI detects slouching, proximity to the screen, and distraction events (looking away, phone usage), and issues gentle audio prompts.

*Partnership mode* enables active homework support: the child can ask questions verbally, request vocabulary dictation, have work checked, or receive explanation of concepts—all via the lamp's voice interface, without requiring a separate device.

### Psychological Healing Layer

This is the platform's most technically differentiated feature set and the one most difficult to replicate through hardware-only approaches.

The core mechanism is **non-inductive triggering**: rather than explicitly asking a child "how are you feeling?"—which produces unreliable responses—the system passively monitors conversational signals, behavioral patterns (energy level, response latency, topic avoidance), and usage deviations over time. The Psychology Vertical Model processes these signals to surface potential emotional states: anxiety around exams, social withdrawal, low mood, or stress responses.

Once a signal is detected, the system generates **personalized healing content**:
*   Ambient lighting mode shifts toward warm, calming profiles
*   TTS-delivered micro-stories or guided breathing narration matched to the detected emotional state
*   Adaptive music suggestions via the music plugin
*   For persistent signals: parent notification via the companion app

The term used internally is **"invisible healing"**—interventions that occur through the ambient environment without requiring the child to engage with a wellness app or acknowledge a problem.

### Deployed Case Studies

#### Case 01 — Arrow (箭牌) Smart Lighting

Arrow, a leading Chinese lighting brand, deployed Agility AI in its floor lamp and desk lamp product lines. Key implementations:

*   **Customizable AI Assistant Personas**: Purchasers can name and configure the voice assistant's personality—selecting from multiple voice timbres and behavioral profiles (energetic, calm, professional). The assistant responds to a custom wake word.
*   **Voiceprint Management**: Family members are registered by voiceprint, allowing the system to switch between child mode, parent mode, and guest mode automatically based on who is speaking.
*   **Plugin Ecosystem**: Weather, music streaming, news briefings, and third-party content plugins extend functionality without firmware updates. New plugins are activated via the companion mini-program OTA.

#### Case 02 — Yasheng (亚晟) Hotel: Healing Art Frame

A hospitality application that dissolves the boundary between lighting, art, and aromatherapy. The Yasheng installation places a smart art frame in hotel rooms combining three elements:

*   **Generative digital art display**: The frame cycles through curated ambient artworks, shifting palette and content based on time of day and guest interaction
*   **Aromatherapy synchronization**: Scent diffusion is linked to the ambient lighting mode via smart-home plugin, creating a multi-sensory environment without manual operation
*   **Ambient lighting strip control**: LED strips around the room are orchestrated through the same app interface as the art frame, allowing guests to configure complete room ambiance from a single control point

The companion mobile app provides guests with full scene control, preset "moods" (deep sleep, energize, focus, romantic), and a manual override for every element.

### The Agility AI Thesis for Lighting

The lighting industry entered the AI era later than consumer electronics, which means first-mover advantages are still available. Agility's architecture is designed to be the software layer that any hardware manufacturer can deploy: the IoT management APIs abstract away hardware differences, the multi-LLM routing makes model provider decisions reversible, and the vertical models are assets that compound with usage data over time. For hardware brands with established distribution but limited AI software capability, this represents a faster path to competitive parity—and a more defensible product—than building model training in-house.
